Things People Ask Us

Q: Is the first lesson really free?

A: Yes, the first lesson is actually free. If you decide that we’re not a good fit, you don’t owe us anything. This extends to cancellations too: if you cancel our first lesson, we won’t come hounding you for any sort of cancellation fee.


Q: What grade levels do you teach?

A: We have tutors for students in grades 6-12, as well as diagnostic tests and study materials so your child can focus on learning what they need to know


Q: Do you have SAT/ACT prep?

A: Unfortunately, we don’t currently offer SAT or ACT prep courses, but if you’re interested, we suggest Khan Academy as a free alternative. If you want a more personalized experience, The Princeton Review offers some of the most comprehensive prep courses in the world, though they can get a little pricey .


Q: How old are your tutors?

A: Our tutors are all high-achieving and personally trained high school students; we don’t believe you need a college degree to teach Algebra. This also helps our tutors connect with their students by talking with them, instead of down to them. .


Q: Is there a cancellation policy?

A: After the first lesson, yes. If you cancel the lesson within 24 hours of its start time, we will bill you for half of the cost of the lesson, and if you cancel within 1 hour, you will owe the whole cost of the lesson. We offer a respectful and productive relationship between tutor and student, and last minute cancellations show a lack of respect for the tutor’s time.
